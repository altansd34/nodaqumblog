Mirtha Jung's bio

Mirtha was born on 3 December 1952 in Cuba. Her upbringing was spent with her parents in Cuba, where she attended a local high school. After completing her high school education, she worked as a waitress until she met and eventually married George Jung.

Career

Professionally, Mirtha is a poet, entrepreneur, and writer. She rose to fame following the release of the movie Blow in 2001. The film was about her drug smuggler ex-husband, George Jung. In the movie, Mirtha's role was played by Penelope Cruz, while Johnny Depp played the role of George.

Mirtha was also famously known as a drug smuggler, a job her husband introduced to her. The two smuggled drug substances for a long time before breaking up. Many believe she had a significant role in her husband's drug trade business in the late '70s and '80s.

What happened to Mirtha Jung?

It is said that Mirtha was involved and helped George Jung in smuggling illegal substances to America from Colombia. A few years into the business, she was caught and imprisoned for several years. After her release from prison, she changed her ways and had a stable career.

Who is Mirtha Jung married to?

Young Mirtha Jung met George in Colombia through one of his Colombian friends. She was just 24 years old at the time, and she was ten years younger than George. They dated each other for a few years before they got married in 1977.

They were blessed with a beautiful daughter on 1 August 1978. She loved George dearly, but their relationship soured a couple of years after their marriage. Their drug-related lives destroyed their marriage, leading to a divorce in 1984.

Who did Mirtha Jung marry?

After the divorce it is unclear whether Mirtha remarried or found another husband after her divorce. On the other hand, George married Ronda Clay Spinelli. George died in May 2021.
